Hello, my name is Susan

I initially trained and worked as a Graphic Designer and Photographer. More recently, I have been studying computing and learning coding. I like to give back to the community through voluntary work and have a strong commitment to equality for women.

1. Coding

I find pair coding and discussing solutions in a group an energising and productive learning experience. I enjoy interesting problems, devising a range of approaches and finding elegant solutions – combining creativity and systematic logic.

2. Education: Media Studies

As a volunteer consultant, I develop curriculum resources and maintain a blog to support photography, magazines and design for Media Studies. I train staff to support practical production coursework.

3. Photography

Alongside volunteering, I was a Freelance Photographer for London College of Fashion (UAL). Prior to this, I had established a photographic studio, working as Photographer and Art Director for a publisher.

4. Volunteering

Previously, I volunteered for a North Islington community centre. I developed their website, trained staff and developed documentation for their content management system.

5. Graphic Design

My initial career was in Graphic Design, designing books, magazines and other materials. I organised and directed photoshoots and became more involved in that area, which lead to becoming an in-house photographer.

6. Equality

Additionally, during my early career, I volunteered at co-operatively-run magazines such as Spare Rib and the Riot GRRL magazine, Bad Attitude. I worked in design at City Limits and Everywoman.
